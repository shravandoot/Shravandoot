The Communication Struggle for Deaf Children

Deaf children (ages 5–17) face real-time communication challenges in schools, homes, and public spaces. This leads to social isolation, emotional struggles, and educational setbacks. Existing tools are often expensive, bulky, and not child-friendly. There’s a critical need for an affordable, portable, and eco-friendly solution to bridge this gap

Our Solution

Our solution is a pair of smart glasses designed to bridge the communication gap for deaf children. The glasses use a transparent OLED display to convert spoken words into real-time text, ensuring easy communication in any setting. Built with eco-friendly, recycled materials, the lightweight design is durable and comfortable for everyday use. Cost-efficient components like the ESP32 processor and MEMS microphone keep the price under ₹1500, making it accessible to more families. A built-in blink detection system enables hands-free interaction, adding convenience. Compact and portable, the glasses are tailored to meet the needs of children aged 5 to 17.

Our Journey

14 July 2024

Concept Development

Started by making a hologram with the idea of developing smart glasses where the holographic effect is projected onto the lens. Identified the problem of deaf communication barriers in India.

3 August 2024

First Prototype

Built the first prototype using an OLED screen and Arduino Nano. Focused on making the text display clear for effective visual communication.

28 September 2024

Cloud Computing Attempt

Integrated Raspberry Pi for enhanced processing but faced performance issues. Decided to switch to cloud computing to offload processing.

10 November 2024

System Reset Issue

Accidentally reset the entire system while installing Ubuntu, leading to data loss. Rebuilt everything from scratch, improving efficiency along the way.

2 January 2025

Final Prototype

Overcame software and hardware challenges to build the final prototype. The device is now fully functional, featuring real-time speech-to-text display.

Future Improvements

Future versions of the glasses will feature AI-powered contextual responses with blink-to-select functionality, enhancing interaction. Customizable text options and tone detection will cater to diverse age groups and communication needs. Energy efficiency improvements, like solar-powered batteries, will extend usage time while being eco-friendly. With global language support, the glasses aim to transform communication for millions of children worldwide.
